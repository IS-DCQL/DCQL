# Threats to Validity — Usability and Learning-Curve Experiment (§6.4 / RQ2)

This note records the threats to validity for the controlled cross-language
usability and learning-curve experiment reported in §6.4 (RQ2) of the paper
(49 participants, 7 groups; scores, completion time, error patterns, and
two-week memory retention).

## Internal validity

Participants' prior experience may influence the usability results. Although we
controlled for comparable computer-science backgrounds, individual differences
may remain, and the order of teaching materials and tasks could affect learning
outcomes. We mitigated these risks through a uniform teaching protocol and
counterbalanced task assignment, but residual effects cannot be fully excluded.

## External validity

The student participants do not fully represent practising scientists or
professional developers, and the four scientific domains cannot cover all types
of scientific data, so the results may not generalize without further study.
In addition, each language group has only seven participants, which limits the
statistical power of the comparison.

## Construct validity

Lines of code, syntactic noise, and Halstead complexity are proxies for
syntactic complexity and do not fully capture usability, and exam scores and
completion time do not cover all real query scenarios.
